Map of Europe

by Pim van Alphen, 11082887

Information: I used a multihue color scale ranging from orange to blue (hex: #d73027, #fc8d59, #fee090, #e0f3f8, #91bfdb, #4575b4). The colors were selected using Color Brewer. I chose a 6 classes diverging color scale, while keeping the "colorblind safe" option enabled. the colors represent an ordinal scale of population, which is described hereafter. As we can readily see the colors in the middle of the scale are the brightest, they stand out less than the colors on the edge of the scale. For reading the values in the middle of the range I suggest to try selecting them. I tried to emphasise the map on the edges of the scale, which are easier distinguished because of their intense colors. Since the focus is on the edges of the scale, I made the ranges relatively bigger towards them. Ratios between minimum and maximum value of classes range from 2 in the middle to 10 at the edges of the scale. I left non-european countries out of the map, although Russia and Turkey are displayed to keep the map intact. Those countries are situated in Asia and Europe.

Population: less than 100.000, 100.000 to 1.000.000, 1.000.000 to 5.000.000, 5.000.000 to 10.000.000, 10.000.000 to 50.000.000, more than 50.000.000.
minimum value: 33000 (San Marino)*, maximum value: 81276000 (Germany)**
Andorra (between France and Spain), San Marino (in the north of Italy) and Monaco (too small to be visible on the map) are both in the first class

* the actual country with the least inhibitants of Europe is Vatican City with 800 inhibitants. It is not featured on the map.
** Russia has even bigger population than Germany, 144.031.000 inhibitants, but it is located intercontinental.
